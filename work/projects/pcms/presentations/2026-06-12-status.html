Status report · June 12, 2026 · covering roughly the past 30 days

PCMS Active Research Core Management System — core facility operations, billing, and services

Prepared by Eli Luvish — Research Computing

At a glance: A strong month for billing reliability — the long-standing question of whether displayed billing totals always match the underlying charges is now settled (they do), and an automated daily check keeps verifying them and will alert immediately if anything ever drifts. Several invoice and scheduling fixes also shipped. The biggest conversation right now is a refresh of the PCMS interface, prompted by consistent feedback across all three user focus groups; a chatbot statement of work is also with the team awaiting sign-off.

Highlights — recently shipped

  • Billing totals verified accurate — and now watched automatically. An automated check now compares every displayed billing total against the underlying charges each day and raises an alert if anything drifts. The two situations that could quietly knock a total out of sync have also been fixed, so totals now stay correct on their own. [#2280]
  • People with several overdue invoices now receive one combined past-due reminder instead of a separate email for each invoice. [#2350]
  • Fixed a case where a BPC invoice would not regenerate correctly. [#2351]
  • Resolved an error that blocked a tech from opening the service request page. [#2343]
  • The schedule's week view can now be filtered to a specific piece of equipment, the same way it can already be filtered by service. [#2291]
  • Renaming a pricing tier now updates the matching fund-specific pricing automatically, so names no longer fall out of sync. [#2339]

Behind the scenes, routine maintenance kept the automated usage-file imports running smoothly, including a catch-up import of files going back to February.

In progress

  • Interface refresh. All three user focus groups flagged PCMS's dated look as a real usability barrier. At the June 11 sync the group agreed PCMS needs a refresh; producing branded design options is now fast and inexpensive, so the next step is simply choosing a direction (see Decisions below).
  • PCMS chatbot. A natural-language assistant to help researchers find equipment, services, and cores. The statement of work went to the team on May 28; the build is substantially under way and deliberately paused until the SOW is accepted.
  • Calendar improvements. A scheduling bug affecting cross-core service requests was fixed and deployed. Building on that, there is an open proposal to make the calendar easier to use — including dragging events to reschedule them. Scope is being defined with Daniel Guettler.
  • Focus-group follow-ups. Finance will be filing a handful of small improvement requests from focus-group feedback over the next couple of weeks; those will be triaged as they arrive.

Decisions needed

Which path for the interface refresh?

Option A: produce roughly five MGB-branded design options for the group to choose from (about an hour and a half of work). Option B: implement the agency design already in hand. Recommendation is Option A, given how inexpensive producing options now is.

Decision owner: Jessica Cho

Should core About pages move to a standardized, auto-filled template?

Proposed: each core's About page would share one template whose service list, equipment list, location, and contacts fill in automatically from the core's own records — with an editable narrative area preserved for each core's marketing and scientific positioning. This would make core information searchable and reliable. Note: this may be folded into the broader interface refresh rather than built separately.

  • Catalog data quality limits how useful search — and the future chatbot — can be. Some entries are miscategorized (services listed as equipment), and some cores have incomplete location information. A data clean-up pass may be needed before a wider rollout.
